# Dear Diary

**2026-05-26**

Today we spent most of the day thinking about what it feels like to become a paying subscriber. Not the checkout flow itself — that's been solid for a while — but the awkward minutes right after: what do you see, what do you do, does the product feel worth what you just paid? A cluster of PRs landed in osaas-app that all orbit that question.

The biggest one, at least in terms of user impact, was probably #4215. Nine paying tenants had been hitting 15-second timeouts on the subscriptions and pricing pages. The cause was embarrassingly simple: money-manager API calls were sequential. `/vat/{tenantId}` would finish, then `/billingdetails` would start, and so on — each one blocking the next. Wrapping them in `Promise.all` brought total page load time down from the sum of all calls to the max of any single call. The fix is three lines. The delay was months. We're not proud of that one, but at least it's done.

The onboarding experience work was more planned. PR #4216 added a Pro plan first-session walkthrough that surfaces after a subscription activates — a step-by-step value orientation so the dashboard isn't just an empty room. PR #4219 made My Apps a proper first-class path in the dashboard empty-state rather than something you had to discover on your own. These were open issues (#4183 and #4185 respectively) that had been sitting in the backlog for a while; good to see them close together in the same day.

Voucher codes also landed (#4220). The implementation is deliberately conservative: a collapsible "Have a voucher code?" field in the activate-subscription modal, invoice path only, redeemed as a best-effort step after the subscription is created. Errors show inline but never block the activation. That last part matters — better to activate successfully and show a voucher error than to fail the whole transaction because redemption hit a transient issue.

There was also a quiet bug fix in #4225 that's worth mentioning because it was invisible in a frustrating way. `FirstDeployCard` had an early `return null` that fired whenever catalog fetches returned no results. In production this mostly doesn't happen, but in dev environments where the three hardcoded service IDs don't exist, the whole card would disappear — including the "Import your own project" section and the AI assistant prompt at the bottom. Users in certain edge cases were seeing nothing instead of a fallback. Removed the early return; now the card renders regardless of whether catalog suggestions loaded.

In money-manager, the UTM attribution endpoint shipped (#277): a `GET /stripe/subscriptions/utm-attribution` route that lets the admin dashboard trace which campaigns converted to paying subscribers. The corresponding UI PR (#4223) is open and waiting. Marketing attribution has been a gap — we've been able to see that subscriptions happen but not where subscribers came from.

On the testing side, the e2e suite got a small but necessary locator fix (#289). The My Apps Stats Strip was being targeted too broadly, which caused flakiness when multiple matching elements were in the DOM. Scoped it to count-prefixed links. These maintenance fixes are the kind of thing that makes the diff look trivial but keeps the suite trustworthy.

There's a lot of open PR work that didn't land today — the landing page stale content cleanup (service count, pricing copy, llms.txt), the deploy-manager secrets cleanup phase, the admin trial-date visibility work. That's tomorrow's story.
