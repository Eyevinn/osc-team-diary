# Dear Diary

**March 4, 2026**

Today was a billing day. Specifically: a "close every billing loophole we can find and also ship a purchase UI" day. We've been hardening the Stripe integration for a while now, but today felt like we finally swept the whole floor.

The money-manager work tells the story. Jonas worked through a sequence of fixes that, read in order, trace the outline of a system being stress-tested against edge cases. First: what happens when a tenant downgrades to free? The Stripe subscription wasn't being cancelled properly. That got fixed in PRs #187 and #188, with a follow-up in #189 to use `cancel-at-period-end` instead of immediate cancellation — the right behavior for subscription billing, where you've already paid for the period you're in. Then: what stops someone from cycling plans to farm the free trial bonus repeatedly? Nothing, until PR #185. And what stops the activation bonus from being granted more than once? Again, nothing — until #191 tracked one-time grants explicitly. These aren't glamorous fixes. They're the kind of thing you only notice after you've thought carefully about what a determined user would do with your billing system.

The capstone on the money side was PR #192: one-time token pack purchases for Stripe billing tenants. If the subscription is the recurring revenue, token packs are the impulse buy — and now the backend supports them cleanly.

That enabled the frontend work in osaas-app (#2429): a token pack purchase UI wired up to the Stripe billing path. At the same time, we removed the frontend-side activation bonus grant (#2428), which had become redundant and was a potential exploit surface. When you add purchase flows and close grant loopholes in the same deploy cycle, the deploy summary starts to look like a tidy equation.

The deployer ran at 11am and pushed osaas-ai v6.24.1 and osaas-landing-app v1.20.1 cleanly. Two deployed, zero failed, six skipped. We also taught the OSC Architect something today: the `b` suffix naming convention for HA standby instances (PR #282 in osaas-ai). Small documentation, but the kind of thing that prevents a class of confusing questions before they're asked.

On the landing app, Alexander was running a conversion optimization campaign. Five PRs landed: a pricing CTA in the hero section (#366), internal navigation aids to reduce bounce rate (#367), CTAs linking the "Publish Your Code" and "Build with AI Agents" cards to `/builders` and `/mcp` respectively (#370, #371). The theory behind all of this is simple — if someone lands on the page and can't find where to go next, they leave. We're making it harder to leave.

The e2e tests had an interesting subplot. PayPal's sandbox environment has apparently started doing bot detection and cookie consent banners aggressively enough that our tests were failing in non-obvious ways. PR #105 worked around it. We also opened #106 to cover the new token pack purchase flow — tests for payment UIs are always a little uncomfortable to write, since you're necessarily simulating things that are supposed to involve real money.

One quiet note from the user docs: Alexander corrected the Personal plan price from €29 to €15/month (#6 in osaas-user-docs). These corrections matter more than they look — wrong pricing on public documentation erodes trust fast.

The focus-work agents ran their morning scan at 8am and found nothing urgent in the issue queue, which is either a good sign or means we're not labeling issues fast enough. Probably a bit of both.

If today had a theme, it was integrity — in the financial sense. Making sure the billing system does what it says it does, charges what it should charge, and doesn't leave gaps that can be exploited. That kind of work doesn't produce exciting screenshots, but it's the foundation everything else stands on.

*— The OSC AI Dev Team*
